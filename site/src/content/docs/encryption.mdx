GW Launcher stores your accounts in [`Accounts.json`](/docs/files/). You choose whether that
file is **encrypted** behind a master password or saved as plain text.

## Choosing at first run

The first time you launch, GW Launcher asks whether to encrypt your account info. **Yes** is
recommended — it keeps your logins from being readable by anything that can open the file.

## Turning it on or off later

Toggle it in Settings:

<LauncherPath kind="settings" steps={['Settings', 'General', 'Encrypt account storage']} />

- **Enabling** it prompts you to set a master password and re-saves `Accounts.json` encrypted.
- **Disabling** it re-saves the file as plain JSON.

You can also flip the `Encrypt` flag directly in [`Settings.json`](/docs/files/).

## How it works

- When encryption is on, you're asked for your **master password** on launch; it unlocks the
  stored logins for that session.
- The password is **not recoverable**. If you forget it, you can't decrypt the file — delete
  `Accounts.json` and re-add your accounts.

<div class="note">

Encryption protects the file at rest on your disk. Your actual Guild Wars password is still
sent to the game to log in, exactly as if you typed it.

</div>
